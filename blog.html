Understanding Your Brain's Reticular Activating System (RAS)

The Reticular Activating System (RAS) is a crucial neural network in our brain that acts as a sophisticated filter, processing millions of sensory inputs to determine what reaches our conscious awareness. Located in the brainstem, this remarkable system allows only about 40 bits of information through from the 11 million bits detected every second. The RAS can be intentionally programmed through clear goal-setting, visualization, and positive affirmations to enhance focus and recognize opportunities aligned with our objectives. Understanding and working with your RAS can dramatically improve success rates by helping filter out distractions while maintaining attention on what truly matters.
